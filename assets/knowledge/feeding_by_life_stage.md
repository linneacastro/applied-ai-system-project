# Feeding Frequency by Life Stage

Feeding schedules vary significantly based on a pet's age and species. Getting this right matters because underfeeding young animals stunts growth, while overfeeding adults and seniors leads to obesity and joint problems.

## Puppies and Kittens (under 6 months)

Very young pets need frequent small meals because their stomachs are small and their energy demands are high. Puppies under 4 months should eat 3 to 4 times per day. Kittens under 4 months should eat 3 to 4 times per day as well. From 4 to 6 months, both can typically move to 3 meals per day. Each feeding is short — usually 5 to 10 minutes of active eating — but the total daily time commitment can be 20 to 40 minutes across all meals.

## Adult Dogs and Cats (1 to 7 years)

Most adult dogs do well on 2 meals per day, spaced roughly 8 to 12 hours apart. Adult cats can be fed 2 meals per day or given measured portions for grazing, depending on their eating style. Free-feeding is not recommended for dogs or for cats prone to overeating. A typical adult feeding takes 5 to 15 minutes.

## Senior Pets (7+ years for dogs, 10+ for cats)

Senior pets often benefit from smaller, more frequent meals — 2 to 3 per day — to support digestion and maintain stable energy. Appetite changes are common and should be monitored. Seniors with dental issues may need softened food, which takes slightly longer to prepare.

## Key Takeaway

Feeding is a high-priority daily task. The number of feedings per day and total time required depend heavily on age. A schedule that works for an adult dog will undersupply a puppy.
