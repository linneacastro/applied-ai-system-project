# Vet Checkups and Preventive Care

Veterinary care is not something that happens only when a pet is sick. Preventive checkups catch problems early and keep vaccinations and parasite protection current. Missing routine care is the single most common cause of avoidable health issues.

## Puppies and Kittens

Young pets need a series of vet visits in their first 4 months:

- **First visit**: 6 to 8 weeks old. Physical exam, deworming, first vaccinations (DHPP for puppies, FVRCP for kittens).
- **Second visit**: 10 to 12 weeks. Boosters.
- **Third visit**: 14 to 16 weeks. Final core vaccines plus rabies.
- **Spay or neuter**: typically 4 to 6 months, though some vets recommend waiting longer for large-breed dogs.

Each visit is usually 30 to 45 minutes plus travel time.

## Adult Pets (1 to 7 years)

Healthy adult dogs and cats should have an annual wellness exam. This includes a physical, weight check, dental check, and vaccine boosters as needed (typically every 1 to 3 years depending on the vaccine). Most adult visits take 30 minutes. Heartworm testing and a fecal exam are usually annual as well.

## Senior Pets (7+ years for dogs, 10+ for cats)

Senior pets benefit from **twice-yearly** wellness exams because health changes faster in older animals. Bloodwork is typically recommended annually to catch kidney, liver, and thyroid issues early. Plan for a longer visit — 45 to 60 minutes — because the exam is more thorough.

## Ongoing Preventive Care (Monthly)

- **Flea and tick prevention**: monthly, year-round in most climates. Takes 1 to 2 minutes to administer.
- **Heartworm prevention**: monthly for dogs, often recommended for cats in high-risk areas. 1 to 2 minutes per dose.

## Key Takeaway

Vet visits are low-frequency but high-priority tasks. Missing a puppy vaccine visit can leave a pet unprotected against deadly diseases; missing a senior wellness exam can let a treatable condition progress to an emergency.
