# Grooming Intervals by Coat Type

Grooming frequency depends more on coat type than on species. Skipping grooming causes matting (which is painful and can hide skin problems), excessive shedding, and in some breeds, overheating or impaired mobility.

## Short-Coated Pets

**Dogs** — labradors, beagles, boxers, dachshunds, greyhounds. A brief brushing 1 to 2 times per week, about 5 to 10 minutes each, is enough. Baths every 4 to 8 weeks, or when visibly dirty.

**Cats** — domestic shorthairs, Siamese, Burmese. Many short-haired cats groom themselves adequately, but a weekly brushing of 5 minutes reduces hairballs and shedding. Baths are rarely needed.

## Medium and Long-Coated Pets

**Dogs** — golden retrievers, collies, Australian shepherds, spaniels. Need brushing 3 to 4 times per week, 10 to 15 minutes per session, to prevent tangles. Professional grooming every 6 to 8 weeks is common.

**Cats** — Persians, Maine coons, ragdolls, Himalayans. Need daily brushing of 5 to 10 minutes. Mats form quickly in long coats and are difficult to remove without shaving.

## Double-Coated Pets

Breeds like huskies, German shepherds, Samoyeds, and Pomeranians have a dense undercoat that sheds heavily twice a year. During shedding seasons (spring and fall), expect daily brushing of 15 to 20 minutes. Off-season, 2 to 3 times per week is typically sufficient. **Do not shave double-coated dogs** — it damages the coat's insulation.

## Nails, Teeth, and Ears

Nail trims every 3 to 4 weeks for most pets. Tooth brushing is ideal 2 to 3 times per week, 2 to 5 minutes per session. Ear cleaning varies by breed — floppy-eared dogs (spaniels, retrievers) need weekly checks; erect-eared breeds rarely need cleaning.

## Key Takeaway

Grooming is often underestimated in time budgets. A long-coated pet can require 30+ minutes of grooming per day during heavy shedding, which competes directly with walks and play for limited owner time.
