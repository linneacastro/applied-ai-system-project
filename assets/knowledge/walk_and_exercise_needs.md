# Walk and Exercise Needs

Exercise requirements depend on species, size, breed energy level, and age. A mismatched exercise routine causes behavior problems in under-exercised dogs and joint damage in over-exercised puppies or seniors.

## Dogs by Size

**Small dogs (under 20 lbs)** — chihuahuas, pugs, dachshunds, small terriers. Typically need 30 to 45 minutes of exercise per day. This can be split into two shorter walks of 15 to 20 minutes each, or one longer walk plus indoor play.

**Medium dogs (20 to 50 lbs)** — beagles, border collies, cocker spaniels, Australian shepherds. Usually need 45 to 60 minutes per day, often higher for herding or working breeds. Two walks of 25 to 30 minutes is a common pattern.

**Large dogs (50 to 90 lbs)** — labradors, golden retrievers, German shepherds. Typically need 60 to 90 minutes per day. Working and sporting breeds in this range may need closer to 2 hours.

**Giant dogs (over 90 lbs)** — great danes, mastiffs, saint bernards. Counterintuitively, giant breeds often need less intense exercise than large breeds — 45 to 60 minutes is often enough. Long walks are better than high-impact running because of joint strain.

## Life Stage Adjustments

Puppies should not be force-exercised. A common guideline is 5 minutes of structured exercise per month of age, up to twice a day, until growth plates close (around 12 to 18 months depending on breed). Senior dogs often need shorter, gentler walks — 15 to 30 minutes, twice per day — with attention to pace and terrain.

## Cats

Indoor cats need 15 to 30 minutes of active play per day, typically split into 2 or 3 sessions. Interactive toys (wand toys, laser pointers) are more effective than passive toys left out.

## Key Takeaway

Walk duration is not one-size-fits-all. A 60-minute walk is appropriate for a healthy adult lab but dangerous for a 4-month-old puppy of the same breed.
